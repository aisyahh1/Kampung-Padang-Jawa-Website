Recognized interchangeably as steamed cotton bread or filled steamed bread, this popular street food establishment is situated in the heart of Kampung Padang Jawa. This locally originated stall has gained prominence for its meticulously crafted homemade fluffy roti. The recipe encompasses a fusion of Chinese bao and indigenous Malay culinary influences. The freshly prepared roti is generously filled with a variety of sweet and savory sauces, allowing patrons the freedom to customize their selections according to personal preferences. Many customers opt to complement their roti with sauces featuring beef and chicken meats. Alternatively, some patrons favor diverse sauce options such as onde-onde, popcorn caramel, and butterscotch. The stall's location is at Kampung Padang Jawa, 41300 Shah Alam, Selangor, and it operates from Tuesday to Sunday.

Pecal Lele offers Indonesian cuisine with a focus on promoting ayam penyet as its signature dish, and it operates around the clock. Renowned as a favored local destination for residents of Padang Jawa and nearby UiTM students, the stall is situated at Lot 617, Jalan Berangan, Kampung Padang Jawa, 40200 Shah Alam, Selangor. The ayam penyet rice is affordably priced at RM4.50, and the fried catfish (Pecal Lele) is available for RM5.50.

Apam Balik Kuhot, nestled in Kampung Padang Jawa, stands out as a culinary treasure, encapsulating the vibrant essence of Malaysian street food. Esteemed for its expertise in preparing the quintessential Apam Balik, a traditional pancake brimming with delightful nuances, this beloved local establishment has gained widespread acclaim. What distinguishes Apam Balik Kuhot is its distinctive array of fillings. Ranging from the timeless blend of peanuts and sugar to inventive selections such as Nutella, cheese, or creamy corn, the stall provides a diverse spectrum of flavors catering to a wide range of tastes from 21 different flavours.
